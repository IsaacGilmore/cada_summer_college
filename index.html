Some Art to Shape Our Thinking:

Jean Tinguely

Homage to New York (1960)

"During its brief operation, a meteorological trial balloon inflated and burst, colored smoke was discharged, paintings were made and destroyed, and bottles crashed to the ground. A player piano, metal drums, a radio broadcast, a recording of the artist explaining his work, and a competing shrill voice correcting him provided the cacophonic sound track to the machine’s self-destruction—until it was stopped short by the fire department."

Famous New Media Artist Jeremy Bailey

The Web I Want (2015)

"Famous New Media Artist Jeremy Bailey wears the internet, exploring body objectification online by digital augmenting his own body. A new online performance for Southbank Centre's Web We Want Festival."

Angela Washko

Safety (Sea Change): An Interview in World of Warcraft (2015)

Instead of killing enemies and getting badass equipment like she used to, she started traveling to major towns to discuss the ways in which women are treated in the game-space with other players. This led to longer discussions about feminism with players from geographically varied places meeting together in this virtual but spatial and representative corporatized public space. The conversations she facilitated are documented and reproduced in videos and images online. More recently she started performing the process live for audiences, projecting her screen and doing live-narration of the conversations as they unfold.



How can we take this mentality and think about our tools of art making?

Can digital communication tools (like Zoom and Social Media) be seen as a possibility instead of obstacle?

Can we rethink the capability of tools like Zoom and use them for art making?

Not just using the platforms to make art, but making art that thinks about them?

In the same way that Tinguely made auto-destructive sculptures to question the gallery, can we make art that questions platforms like Zoom, Instagram, Tick Tock, etc.?

Exercise for Today:

  • You'll be in a group of 4-5
  • You'll have until 11:00 to work together
  • The thing you make could take many different forms:
    • A video (Zoom recording, Tick Tock, Instagram video, etc.)
    • Image(s)
    • A live zoom performance
    • Something else of your imagination
  • Collectively write 100-200 words describing the thing you made
    • (Some questions to think through)
      • What did you make?
      • How are you thinking differently about the medium you're using?
      • What theme are you trying to express?
      • What would you do if you had more time?
  • Submit your creation as well as your 100-200 words to Box

Some thoughts to get you thinking:

What are the expectations of the platform?

How can we subvert, or go against, those expectations?

What if you don't speak, or constantly speak?

What is the format of the image or video?

How can we do something unexpected?

How can the space be used in a counterproductive way?

Who (or what) is supposed to be visible?

What can you say by changing who or what is visible?

What if you're visible more than once?

Does something different happen if you move closer or further from the camera?

What is the platform meant to do?

Is there something you want it to do that it isn't supposed to do?
